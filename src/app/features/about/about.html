Signs of Hearing Loss

Recognizing the early signs and taking action can make all the difference in your quality of life.

tv

TV or radio volume

Others continuously comment that you set the volume too loud.

groups

Group conversations

Difficulty following discussions involving two or more people.

noise_aware

Noisy environments

Confusion and trouble focusing in restaurants, malls, or meetings.

visibility

Relying on visuals

Depending on reading lips or closely watching faces as people talk.

volume_mute

Muffled sounds

Normal, everyday sounds continuously appear muffled or distant.

family_restroom

High-pitched voices

Having distinct difficulty hearing women's and children's voices.

question_mark

Unrelated answers

Providing comments or answers out of context in conversations.

notifications_active

Tinnitus

Experiencing a continuous ringing or buzzing sensation in the ears.

medication

Ear Drainage

Noticeable fluid, blood, or pus draining from the ear canal.

pulmonology

Physical Pain

Experiencing sharp aches, throbbing, or continuous pressure inside the ear.

balance

Vertigo or Dizziness

Feeling unbalanced or experiencing sudden spells of severe dizziness.

trending_down

Sudden Hearing Loss

A rapid and noticeable decrease in hearing capability over hours or days.

mood_bad

Frustration & Irritability

Feeling impatient or easily annoyed when struggling to understand conversations.

person_off

Social Withdrawal

Avoiding friends, family, and group settings to escape the strain of listening.

battery_alert

Listening Fatigue

Experiencing severe mental or physical exhaustion at the end of the day.

psychology_alt

Anxiety

Worrying about missing important sounds like alarms, warnings, or phone calls.
